Swimlane.

How to read this The horizontal axis is product-stage, not literal time. Each lane is a stakeholder. Each cell shows what that stakeholder is doing during that phase — and where cells are empty, it means that stakeholder isn't active in that phase. The handoffs between lanes (Phase → Phase) are the conversion mechanisms.

02 — Handoffs

Where lanes cross.

The most important moments in the swimlane are where one stakeholder becomes another. Each handoff is a conversion mechanism — and each one is either a designed moment or an accidental one. Designed moments win.

HANDOFF 01
Viewer → Visitor

Watermark + end-of-deck CTA

The viewer reaches the end of a shared deck. The watermark and the overlay CTA convert curiosity into a homepage visit. Highest-volume conversion path in the entire product.

Mechanism — End-of-deck overlay · Referrer-aware landing
HANDOFF 02
Visitor → Customer (Free)

Live demo + magic first upload

Visitor lands, tries the demo without signup, sees it work, signs up. Then on first upload, the artifact renders perfectly — the moment that earns retention.

Mechanism — Live demo on homepage · Zero-tutorial onboarding
HANDOFF 03
Customer (Free) → Customer (Pro)

Grandfather countdown

The 3-month free window closes. Grandfather discount creates urgency without coercion. One-click upgrade with saved payment converts at the moment of decision.

Mechanism — T-30/T-15/T-7/T-1 banner cadence · 50% for life
HANDOFF 04
Customer → Advocate

Referral program (V1.5)

Customer recommends Zili to colleagues. Without a structured referral mechanism, this happens organically but isn't measured or rewarded. With a referral program, it becomes a primary growth channel.

Mechanism — V1.5 referral program · 1 month free both sides
HANDOFF 05
Customer → Viewer (their audience)

Every share is a recursive loop

Every customer share creates new viewers. The watermark loop closes here — each share is the input to Handoff 01 again. This is the compounding mechanism that makes Zili a growth product, not just a tool.

Mechanism — Share modal · OG card · Watermark on free tier
HANDOFF 06
Operator → Customer (support)

Reactive support across phases

The operator intervenes when customers hit edge cases — failed uploads, billing issues, DMCA notices, account suspensions. The runbooks and templated comms determine whether this is a quiet competence or a public fire.

Mechanism — Pre-written runbooks · Templated user comms · Audit log
03 — What this view adds

What the swimlane uniquely shows.

Insights that only become visible when stakeholders are placed on a shared horizontal axis, that weren't apparent in the per-stakeholder journey map.

INSIGHT 01

Customer doesn't exist in Phase 1 or 2

Lane 3 (Customer) is empty in Discovery and Evaluation. Every customer originates as a Visitor or a Viewer first. This means the marketing site, the watermark, and the OG card are not "marketing" — they're the actual top of the funnel. They must be a V1 priority, not a V1.5 priority.

INSIGHT 02

Viewer never enters Phases 5 or 6 directly

The Anonymous viewer must convert to Visitor → Customer before they ever appear in Monetisation or Advocacy. Every cell where the Viewer is active must service the conversion to the next phase, or that volume is wasted.

INSIGHT 03

Operator is active in every phase

Unlike the other three lanes, the Operator has no empty cells. Every phase of every other lane has an Operator dependency — monitoring, supporting, intervening. Admin tooling is not a side concern; it's the load-bearing infrastructure for everything else.

INSIGHT 04

Phase 6 has no Viewer or Visitor presence

Advocacy is a closed loop between Customer and Operator. There's no direct path from Visitor → Advocate; they have to traverse the full lane first. This validates the sequencing: get the core Customer journey right before investing in advocacy programs.

INSIGHT 05

Three magic moments cluster in Phase 3

The end-of-deck CTA (Viewer), the first signup OAuth (Visitor), and the first-upload magic (Customer) all sit in Activation. A bad Phase 3 breaks three lanes simultaneously. This is where engineering effort compounds.

INSIGHT 06

Recursive loop visible in Customer Phase 6 → Viewer Phase 1

The diagonal flow from "Customer · Advocacy" back up to "Viewer · Discovery" is the compounding mechanism. Every product decision should be evaluated against whether it strengthens or weakens this diagonal arrow. If it doesn't strengthen it, it competes with it.
