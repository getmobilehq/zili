Zili.

Editorial minimal. A frame for content, not the content itself.

— Brand Essence

Zili is the last-mile presenter for LLM-generated artifacts. The interface recedes so the artifact can speak. Typography carries the personality. Negative space carries the confidence. A single warm accent carries the action.

01 — Principles

Five rules we don't break.

These are the constraints that make the brand cohere. When in doubt, return to these.

01

Fidelity is sacred.

The user's artifact is the picture. Our UI is the frame. Never style anything that competes with the rendered content.

02

Restraint over polish.

One accent color. One serif moment per view. No second-order decoration. Subtraction is the design move.

03

Editorial, not corporate.

We borrow from book design and broadsheet papers, not from SaaS dashboards. Type leads. Color follows.

04

Warm, not cold.

Pure white and pure black read as generic. Warm paper and warm ink read as considered. Always tilt warm.

05

Confident in silence.

Motion is rare and purposeful. Empty space is intentional. We don't fill the screen because we can.

06 — Anti-patterns

What we never do.

A short list. Each of these would compromise the editorial frame. We don't argue with the list.

✕
Gradients.
Flat color reads as confident. Gradients read as decoration.
✕
Bright or saturated palettes.
Our users' artifacts already carry the color. We don't add more.
✕
Drop shadows beyond 1px.
Depth is suggested with hairline borders and warm tone, not blur.
✕
Rounded-everything (radius > 6px).
Sharp corners are more editorial. Soft corners are more consumer.
✕
Emoji in UI chrome.
Emoji belong to the user's content. Our chrome stays typographic.
✕
Glassmorphism, neumorphism, any -ism.
Aesthetic trends date the product. Editorial minimalism is harder to date.
✕
Illustrations or mascots.
A serious tool for serious work. Personality lives in the wordmark and the prose.
