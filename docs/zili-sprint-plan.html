Zili · MVP Sprint Plan
v0.1 · May 2026

Sprint.

Twelve fortnights from empty repo to public launch. Outside-in. Risk-first. Vertical slices.

— Posture

This plan assumes a serious side bet: roughly 20 productive engineering hours per week, a solo founder doing full-stack work, with selective contractor pulls for design polish, security review, and content. Every sprint produces a deployable, demoable slice. No sprint exists only to "set up infrastructure" — schema and tooling work attach to the feature that needs them.

01 — Assumptions

What this plan assumes.

Stated assumptions are the difference between a plan and a hope. Each item below can be challenged — but it must be challenged explicitly, not silently revised.

Posture

Serious side bet

Solo founder, ~20 productive engineering hours/week. Not full-time. Not weekends-only. Sprints scoped accordingly.

Cadence

2-week sprints

Fortnight ending Friday. Demo Friday afternoon. Plan next sprint Monday. Twelve sprints = ~24 weeks to launch.

Capacity

~30 points per sprint

Story points calibrated to roughly half-day per point. Capacity = 30 points × 12 sprints = 360 points total.

Sequencing

Outside-in, risk-first

Marketing site + ingest pipeline first. Hard, unknown things tackled in Sprint 1. Vertical slices every fortnight.

Quality bar

Slice-then-deepen

Every sprint produces a deployable slice. Polish is deferred but never forgotten. No "infrastructure sprints" allowed.

Contractors

Selective pulls

Design polish at S4 and S10. Security review at S9. Content/copy at S11. Budgeted in §07 below.

~$8-12k total
02 — Stack

Choices, locked.

Each row picks one technology and commits to it. Lock these in S0 (week before Sprint 1). Changing the stack mid-flight is the single most common reason solo projects miss their launch window.

Framework
Next.js 14+ (App Router)
SSR for shared links. Edge-runtime for OG images. Mature.
Hosting
Vercel
Zero-config edge. Faster ship velocity. Migrate later if cost grows.
Database
Supabase (Postgres)
Postgres + auth + storage in one. Cheap to start. No vendor lock.
Auth
Supabase Auth
Bundled with DB. Google OAuth + email in one provider. One less account.
Object storage
Cloudflare R2
S3-compatible. No egress fees. Better economics at any scale.
Renderer
Sandboxed iframe
Strict CSP. sandbox="allow-scripts" only. Asset proxy through Workers.
Payments
Stripe
Industry default. Stripe Billing for subscriptions. Webhook-driven state.
Analytics
PostHog (self-hosted later)
Funnels, cohorts, feature flags in one. Generous free tier.
Error monitoring
Sentry
Free for solo. Source-map upload. Replay for the hard bugs.
Admin UI
Retool
Built on Postgres direct. Saves weeks of custom-admin work. ~$10/mo.
Email
Resend
React Email components. Cheap. Good deliverability. One-line SDK.
Asset proxy / CSP enforcement
Cloudflare Workers
For asset allow-list, CSP injection, edge caching of shared decks.

04 — The plan

Twelve fortnights.

Three milestones, four sprints each. Each milestone ends with a working artifact that can be demoed to a real user. Every sprint has explicit tasks, points, a Definition of Done, and a single demo deliverable.

05 — Risks

What can break this plan.

Risks are not a "if this happens" list — they're an "expect this to happen, plan for it" list. Each has a mitigation. If a mitigation isn't ready, the risk is unmitigated.

RISK 01

Fidelity test fails in S1

Critical · High likelihood

The visual-diff harness flags >2% pixel drift on too many reference artifacts. Either the sandbox is too restrictive or the CSP is blocking necessary CDNs.

MitigationDefer S2 by one sprint and dedicate time to widening the asset allow-list and tuning iframe permissions. Better one slipped sprint than a broken core thesis.
RISK 02

Mode-detection accuracy below 90%

High · Medium

S2's 25-artifact test set might reveal that the heuristic-based mode detection misclassifies more than 10% of inputs.

MitigationLean on the manual override UX in S2.5 to reduce the cost of false negatives. Track misclassification on real beta uploads and re-tune in S7.
RISK 03

Solo founder burnout

Critical · Medium

20h/week over 24 weeks of consistent context-switching with a day job is the failure mode of most serious side bets.

MitigationStrict sprint cadence with mandatory demo Fridays. Two "rest week" sprints allowed in the plan (S6 and S10 are protected at 26 pts, below capacity). No sprint exceeds 30 committed points.
RISK 04

Security finding requires re-architecture

High · Medium

S9 security review may surface a critical issue with the iframe sandbox or CSP. Worst case: redesign the renderer.

MitigationRun a smaller pre-review at end of S5 with a single security-savvy friend to catch obvious issues early. Reserve S12 buffer if the formal review surfaces work.
RISK 05

Alpha doesn't share

Medium · Medium

S4 ends with 5 alpha users but only one has shared a link. The product is being used but the loop isn't firing.

MitigationPre-recruit alpha users who have a use case that demands sharing (consultants delivering to clients). If still no shares, treat as a real product signal and re-design the share moment in S6.
RISK 06

Stripe billing edge cases

Medium · High

The grandfather lifetime discount is custom logic. Stripe's coupon system handles 90% of cases — the other 10% (downgrade then re-upgrade, plan switches mid-cycle, refunds with coupons) are where bugs hide.

MitigationTest 12 explicit scenarios end-to-end in S9 against Stripe test mode. Document each. Make the test suite a regression check.
RISK 07

LLM-platform shipping native presentation

Medium · Low this window

Claude.ai or ChatGPT adds a "present this artifact" button mid-build. The competitive moat narrows.

MitigationShip anyway. Zili's moat is the share graph, the multi-LLM agnosticism, and the operator surface — not the renderer alone. The first integration won't have those.
RISK 08

Launch-day traffic exceeds capacity

Medium · Low

HN front page sends 50k uniques in 4 hours and the ingest pipeline or the rate limits buckle.

MitigationS11.7 load test simulates 1000 concurrent views. Vercel + Cloudflare scale automatically. The likely failure is Supabase free-tier limits — pre-upgrade to Pro before launch.
